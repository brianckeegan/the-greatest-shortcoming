<p>Bartlett's lecture was not an original act of creation; it entered a formation assembled across the first half of the twentieth century. Four currents converge: the institutional eugenics apparatus at Cold Spring Harbor, codified through sterilization regimes after Buck v. Bell; the German science of Lebensraum (Friedrich Ratzel to Rudolf Kjellén to Karl Haushofer); the American conservation movement, sharing donors and personnel with eugenics through Madison Grant and Henry Fairfield Osborn; and the postwar population-concern community — the Population Council, Paul Ehrlich, Garrett Hardin, the Club of Rome.</p>
